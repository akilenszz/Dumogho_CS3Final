Factual Evidence on Climate Change and Plastic Pollution

Global Temperature Rise

Global temperatures have risen by about 1.1°C since the late 1800s due to human activities such as burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes. This rise has led to melting glaciers, rising sea levels, and more extreme weather events worldwide.

Plastic Pollution

Every year, around 8 million tons of plastic enter the oceans globally, threatening marine life and contaminating food chains. In the Philippines, over 2.7 million tons of plastic waste are generated annually, and approximately 20% ends up in the ocean.





Philippines’ Climate Vulnerability

The Philippines is ranked as the 4th most climate-vulnerable nation in the world. Communities face typhoons, floods, and rising sea levels, affecting lives, agriculture, fisheries, and infrastructure. Coastal and low-lying areas are hit hardest, with billions of pesos in damages every year.

Awareness and Small Actions

Even small changes like reducing single-use plastics, practicing proper waste segregation, conserving energy, and supporting tree planting can collectively reduce the effects of climate change. Awareness is the first step toward meaningful action, and informed communities are stronger and more resilient.
