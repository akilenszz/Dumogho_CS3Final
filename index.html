The Problem

General Santos City has been facing increasing problems with plastic pollution and waste management. According to the Asian Development Bank, the Philippines generates over 2.7 million tons of plastic waste annually, and Gensan is one of the major contributors due to its booming fishing and marketplace industries.

Unfortunately, more than 20% of this waste leaks into the ocean, threatening marine biodiversity and the livelihood of thousands of fisherfolk. Left unchecked, this issue will severely affect both the local environment and the city’s economy.

2022 2,600,000 20%

Current Efforts

Local government units have begun implementing plastic bans in markets, requiring reusable bags and containers. Several NGOs have also started community clean-up drives, yet participation rates remain below 50%.

Moreover, coastal barangays have launched “Adopt-a-Shoreline” programs, but funding and manpower issues limit their effectiveness. These efforts, while significant, are not enough to combat the growing crisis.

Where We Come In

EcoGuardians Gensan bridges the gap between local initiatives and youth-driven action. We aim to mobilize students, workers, and residents to actively participate in waste segregation, coastal clean-ups, and recycling campaigns. Together, we can scale these small actions into meaningful change.
