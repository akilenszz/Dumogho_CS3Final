Solutions to Combat Climate Change and Plastic Pollution

Reduce Plastic Use

Encouraging the use of reusable bags, bottles, and containers can drastically cut down plastic waste. By reducing single-use plastics in homes, schools, and markets, communities can prevent millions of tons of waste from reaching oceans each year.

Proper Waste Segregation

Sorting waste into biodegradable, recyclable, and residual categories ensures proper disposal and recycling. Schools, households, and businesses can implement simple segregation systems to prevent plastic from mixing with general waste and polluting the environment.

Community Clean-Ups

Regular clean-up drives at beaches, rivers, and barangays help remove existing waste and raise public awareness. Participation from students, workers, and residents fosters community responsibility and inspires others to take environmental action.

Tree Planting & Conservation

Reforestation and protecting green spaces help absorb CO2, stabilize soil, and reduce flooding. Students, NGOs, and local communities can participate in tree planting programs to restore ecosystems and fight climate change.

Education & Advocacy

Awareness campaigns, workshops, and school programs empower people with knowledge about climate change and waste management. Advocacy encourages sustainable habits and supports policies for environmental protection at the local and national levels.
